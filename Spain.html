About Spain

Studying in Spain offers international students a world-class education, but the admission process can be complex. Our services are here to simplify this journey, guiding you through the requirements for Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctoral programs. Whether you're from the EU or non-EU/EEA, we assist with everything from school certificate recognition (homologación) to preparing for entrance exams and obtaining a student visa. We also help you meet language proficiency requirements for programs in both Spanish and English, and ensure you have the necessary health insurance and proof of financial resources. Let us support you every step of the way to ensure a smooth and successful application to study in Spain.

Requirements

The requirements to study in Spain for international students are generally not difficult to meet

University Admission Requirements

University admission requirements to study in Spain for international students depend on a few factors, such as nationality, level of study, and study program. The general requirements, which most universities ask students to fulfill, are listed in the following section, depending on the cycle of study (whether you are interested in bachelor’s, master’s, or doctoral programs).

Bachelor Programs

For first-cycle studies, students wishing to pursue their education in Spain must have a valid school leaving certificate and be eligible for university entrance in their home country. Depending on the program, prospective first-cycle students in Spain might need to enter specific entrance exams or go through additional courses.

Non-EU/EEA Students

Students who have finished their previous education in countries outside of the EU/EEA, Switzerland, or China (with whom Spain has a bilateral agreement), are eligible to study in Spain when they fulfill specific requirements. Apart from the student visa (which we will elaborate on later in this article), students must meet other academic requirements.

  • Recognition of your school certificate (Homologación)
  • EBAU (selectividad) entry exam

Master Programs

Requirements to study in Spain for international students wishing to enroll in a master’s program include the relevant bachelor’s degree or equivalent. Other requirements depend on the study program and medium of instruction.

Doctoral Programs

For third-cycle studies at universities in Spain, international students must provide the relevant certificates, degrees, or diplomas earned during previous studies. This specifically includes a bachelor’s and master’s degree.

Language Requirements

Language requirements to study in Spain for international students depend on the medium of instruction of the respective study program.

Spanish Language Requirements

For international students wishing to study in a program where the instruction language is Spanish, proof of Spanish language proficiency is required. Usually, students should submit the test results from the DELE test.

English Language Requirements

For programs taught in English, students will need to provide test results (TOEFL, IELTS, etc.).

Documents

To apply for admission to universities in Spain, students must provide the following documents:

  • Valid Passport valid for the duration of your studies in Spain and should be presented with a clear, legible copy
  • High School Diploma or equivalent (for Bachelor's program applicants)
  • Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent (for Master's and Doctoral program applicants)
  • Recognition of Academic Credentials (Homologación) for non-EU/EEA students
  • Transcripts from previous studies (translated into Spanish or English if necessary)
  • Proof of Language Proficiency (Spanish or English, depending on the program)
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) or Resume (for postgraduate applications)
  • Motivation Letter or Statement of Purpose
  • Recommendation Letters (usually for Master's and Doctoral programs)
  • Application Fee

    Most universities require a non-refundable application fee as part of the admissions process. The amount varies depending on the university and the specific program.

Fees

Tuition fees for non-EU students in Spain vary depending on the type of university and program. At public universities, tuition fees typically range from € 800 to €3000 per year for undergraduate and master's programs, with fees being slightly higher for specialized or technical programs. For private universities, the fees can be significantly higher, often ranging from €10,000 to €20,000 per year. Specifically in Andalusia, public university fees tend to be lower compared to other regions, averaging around €5,000 to €8,000 per year for most programs. However, fees for prestigious or specific programs, such as medical or engineering degrees, may exceed these amounts. It’s advisable to check directly with the university for precise fee details based on the program and study level.
