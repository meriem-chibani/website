About Turkey

You can continue your education in Turkish, English or other languages at the universities, where high-quality education is given at a global level, and work in Türkiye to develop your career ou team provides resources to guide all international students who wish to study in Turkey. You’ll find everything you need to know for studying in Turkey such as Turkish Higher Education System, finding the right program that fits to your potentials, applying for a program, resident permits, and living suggestions as an international student in Turkey

description  Requirements

Bachelor Programs

Students are accepted without an examination, only according to their high school diploma grade point average. Generally, the diploma grade point average is 50%, but it varies according to universities. Some universities accept students based on their SAT etc. International exam results. If there is no Turkish or English language proficiency certificate according to the language of instruction, the University conducts a Language exam. If you cannot pass the language exam, you will go to a language preparation course.

Master Programs

There is no need for the YÖS exam. However, you must get high marks in ALES, GRE or GMAT exams (at least 55%). You must also pass the English language proficiency (at least 55%). The duration of the Master is 2 years and Doctorate 4 years. The duration of the Master without thesis degree is 1 year

Doctoral Programs

For foreign students, several requirements must be met for doctoral and integrated doctoral programs. Applicants must hold both a Bachelor's and Master's degree (with a thesis) for Doctorate programs, or just a Bachelor's for Integrated Doctorate programs. In certain cases, both Bachelor's and Master's diplomas are required together. ALES scores are not necessary, but doctoral candidates must achieve at least a 55 score in exams such as YDS, ÜDS, KPDS, TOEFL, or others accepted by ÖSYM. Foreign language proficiency must be in a language other than the candidate's native tongue. Candidates who pass the Turkish language exam (Yunus Emre Institute or TÖMER) must submit a C1 level certificate from Turkish Learning Centers, unless they graduated from Turkey; others will be given up to one year to learn Turkish. A minimum grade average of 2.25 (59.16%) in the 4.0 system is required for doctoral applicants, with a 3.00 GPA needed for integrated doctoral programs. Admission is assessed based on a combination of graduation average (50%) and interview score (50%), and applicants must have at least a 70-point General Achievement Grade to be considered.

language   Language Requirements

The official medium of instruction at the university. Most public universities adopt the Turkish language as the medium of instruction. However, most private universities allow study in English. On the other hand, some universities offer to study in both English and Turkish.

checklist   Documents

To apply for admission to universities in Turkey, students must provide the following documents:

  • A copy of your General Certificate of Secondary Education (GCSE) translated into the university’s official language, whether it is English or Turkish.
  • Transcripts for secondary school (this is not required in all universities).
  • A copy of a valid passport.
  • A recent personal photograph.
  • Language proficiency certificate, such as TOEFL, IELTS, TÖMER, if any. If one of these certificates is not available, the student should appear for a language placement test at the registered university.
  • Some universities require an application fee, which often ranges between 35$ and 300$. This amount is non-refundable if you are not accepted at the university.

attach_money   Fees

Tuition Fees at Public Universities

In Turkey, public universities are very affordable, especially compared to similar institutions in Europe or the United States. International students usually pay between 100 EUR and 4,000 EUR per academic year. Disciplines like Medicine or Engineering can be more expensive.

Tuition Fees at Private Universities

Private Turkish universities are more expensive than public ones. While you can still find academic courses with reasonable tuition, there are also degrees that cost over 20,000 EUR per year.
