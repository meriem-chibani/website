About Romania

Studying in Romania offers international students a high-quality education, with a rich cultural heritage and affordable living costs. The admission process can be simplified with our services, guiding you through the requirements for Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctoral programs. Whether you're from the EU or non-EU/EEA, we assist with everything from school certificate recognition to preparing for entrance exams and obtaining a student visa. We help you meet language proficiency requirements for programs in both Romanian and English, and ensure you have the necessary health insurance and proof of financial resources. Let us assist you every step of the way to ensure a smooth and successful application to study in Romania.

description  Requirements

The requirements to study in Romania for international students are generally not difficult to meet.

University Admission Requirements

University admission requirements to study in Romania for international students depend on a few factors, such as nationality, level of study, and study program. The general requirements, which most universities ask students to fulfill, are listed in the following section, depending on the cycle of study (whether you are interested in bachelor’s, master’s, or doctoral programs).

Bachelor Programs

For first-cycle studies, students wishing to pursue their education in Romania must have a valid school leaving certificate and be eligible for university entrance in their home country. Depending on the program, prospective first-cycle students in Romania might need to enter specific entrance exams or go through additional courses.

Non-EU/EEA Students

Students who have finished their previous education in countries outside of the EU/EEA are eligible to study in Romania when they fulfill specific requirements. Apart from the student visa, students must meet other academic requirements.

  • Recognition of your school certificate
  • Entry exams as per university requirements

Master Programs

Requirements to study in Romania for international students wishing to enroll in a master’s program include the relevant bachelor’s degree or equivalent. Other requirements depend on the study program and medium of instruction.

Doctoral Programs

For third-cycle studies at universities in Romania, international students must provide the relevant certificates, degrees, or diplomas earned during previous studies. This specifically includes a bachelor’s and master’s degree.

language   Language Requirements

Language requirements to study in Romania for international students depend on the medium of instruction of the respective study program.

Romanian Language Requirements

For international students wishing to study in a program where the instruction language is Romanian, proof of Romanian language proficiency is required. Usually, students should submit test results from recognized Romanian language exams.

English Language Requirements

For programs taught in English, students will need to provide test results (TOEFL, IELTS, etc.).

checklist   Documents

To apply for admission to universities in Romania, students must provide the following documents:

  • Filled Application form;
  • Birth certificate – copy and legalized translation;
  • Copy of the ID which states the permanent residence in the country of origin;
  • Copy of the passport;
  • The Application for the issuance of the letter of acceptance to studies – it MUST be filled in with capital letters and all fields are mandatory;
  • High School Graduation Diploma or its equivalent – copy and legalized translation, authenticated (show / hide) by the authorities in the issuing country;
  • Bachelor’s Degree Diploma or its equivalent – copy and legalized translation, authenticated by the authorities in the issuing country (if applicable);
  • Master’s Degree Diploma or its equivalent – copy and legalized translation, authenticated by the authorities in the issuing country (if applicable);
  • Transcript of Records / Diploma Supplement – copy and legalized translation for all years of studies;
  • Language proficiency certificate (minimum level for the Romanian language B1 or CAMBRIDGE, IELTS, TOEFL, DELF, etc. at minimum level B2 for English, French, German) (if applicable);
  • Medical certificate (issued in an international language) to certify that the candidate does not suffer from contagious diseases or from conditions that are incompatible with the prospective profession;
  • File processing fee receipt.

attach_money   Fees

Tuition fees for non-EU students in Romania vary depending on the type of university and program. For public universities, tuition fees typically range from €1,500 to €4,500 per year for undergraduate and master's programs. Private universities may have higher fees, ranging from €5,000 to €10,000 per year, depending on the program.
